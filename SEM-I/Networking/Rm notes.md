Degrees of problem definition
1. Exploratory Research (Unaware of problem)
2. Descriptive Research (Aware of the problem)
3. Casual Research (Problem clearly defined)

	1. **Exploratory Research** 
	- **methodology approach that investigates research questions that have not previously been studied in depth.**
	- Data Collection methods - Interviews, Surveys/polls, focus group discussion.
	- **Initial research conducted to clarify and define the nature of the problem**.
	- Subsequent research is expected in this area.
	 
	2. **Descriptive Research** 
	- **aim to accurately and systematically describe a population**. 
	- It **can answer what, where and how questions, but not why questions.**
	- When to use - when research **focus is to identify characteristics, frequencies, trends and categories**.
	-  Descriptive research question examples 
		- **How has the Amsterdam housing market changed over the past 20 years?**
		- **Do customers of company X prefer product X or product Y?**
	-  How descriptive research is conducted
		- Start with a hypothesis
		- Test hypothesis using statistical techniques
		- Theoretical Background
		- Add value to the existing literature.
	-  Data Collection - **Surveys, Observations, Case studies**.
